Academic Programs

Students at Christ's are pursuing an incredible variety of interests across almost every academic discipline that you can think of. Why do people and animals get addicted to things? How do plants tell the time? How did ancient kings rule their empires? These are just three of the questions that members of our MCR are trying to answer as they pursue research at Cambridge. On this page you can explore some of the events run by the MCR to enable students here to communicate their research.

ERSS

The Emerging Research Seminar Series is the flagship academic event run by the MCR. It is a multidisciplinary event open to the MCR members or Fellows of the College. There are four events each year, one during each term and one in the summer vacation, and every event consists of three talks - two by graduate students and one by A Fellow of the college.
Speaking at the ERSS is a great opportunity to present your work to an interested audience outside of your department. For more information click here.

Talks

Alongside the ERSS, we run a number of different kinds of talks. Library events see graduate students present talks in the Bodley Library (or the 'Old' Library as it's better known to student) which are connected to the changing exhibitions created by the library staff. We are also planning to introduce a series of informal talks - for instance a brief presentation to accompany a movie night, or a roundtable discussion of ongoing research.

Teaching

A number of MCR members are employed by various colleges and faculties to teach undergraduates. To help develop the teaching skills of members, the MCR runs a Teaching Workshop which meets a couple of times each term. The sessions are multi-disciplinary and provide a forum to discuss difficulties that arise in the course of supervising and to develop new strategies for teaching. We are also hoping to tie this in to the programme of talks that we run in the hope of allowing participants to develop presentation and lecturing skills.

Engagement

Research should never just sit within the confines of your own mind. There's no point doing research if you don't attempt to communicate it. The MCR is looking to expand the participation of our students in the public engagement activities run by the university. We will be running our own training days and events and this page will soon be updated with information about how you can get involved in the exciting things going on within the university as a whole.
